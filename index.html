Caydn Baldwin is a growing composer seeking to heal the individual through his beautiful compositions. He began his career bringing a new perspective of beautiful dissonance to pop music but would later dedicate himself to eye opening new age music. His first song, "Why Music," was written his junior year of high school in 2019. He nourished his crave for beauty in music through debuting his first choral piece, "My Soul" at his senior concert. He would later enter both pieces into the 2021 NAfME Composer's Competition where he received outstanding feedback from the judges. Immediately after high school, he left his future opportunities behind to serve a 2-year voluntary service mission in Retalhuleu, Guatemala. During his time there he wrote many songs dedicated to building the faith of his friends, American and Guatemalan alike. All the while, expanding his musical repertoire from English to also Spanish. Upon return home from his mission he would continue composing new pieces, still seeking to reach people at an individual level and heal them musically. He would soon formally release his first official song, "Majesty," onto all music streaming platforms and currently plans to release more songs in the near future.

Piano

Majesty is the first song that Caydn formally released. It was written upon return home from his 2-year voluntary service mission in Guatemala. The music is illustrative of the majestic power that God has on our lives. Majestic in the sense that He is all powerful and can choose to work miracles in our life according to His will and desire. But also, majestic in the sense of beauty. The song is evidently inspired by the beautiful mountaineous terrain of Rio Blanco, Guatemala and Provo, Utah, hence the chosen cover photo for the release. The summits and vallies of the melody vividly illustrate the natural beauty of the mountains, while the jaw-dropping beauty of the climax of the song demonstrate the awing reverence felt towards God, submitting to His majesty.

Choral

My Soul is an original choral piece that Caydn composed his senior year of high school. It blends a new age piano structure with contemporary choral music, all while truly belonging to the hymnal genre. The real beauty of the piece is hidden behind the message and inspiration that came from revelatory experiences that Caydn underwent while coping with the death of his grandfather and best friend, Dennis Schanz. It was debuted at Caydn's senior concert.

The faithful phrase was inspired by his friend and they mutually agreed that he should put a poem to it and then compose music for it. It is an upbeat and friendly song designed to be an uplifting piece of music for all to enjoy.

An interesting approach was taking in the lyric writing where rather than focusing the song about just one girl, he based the song off what he remembered feeling for every girl of he had ever liked that rejected him.

Our Cross is another piece written by Caydn on his mission. In his first transfer, he read Jesus the Christ by James E. Talmage. The beginning of Chapter 35, "Death and Burial" discusses Christ physically carrying the cross down the path towards Calvary. It describes every detail that the scriptures provide about His suffering. The paragraph touched Caydn's heart and he wrote a poem about the experience. It wasn't until a year later, when hearing his friend's tragic story of the metaphorical cross that they carried that he was inspired with the final lyrics and melody for the song. This song serves as an aid to all people as they carry their own cross in life.

Why Music? is the first song that Caydn wrote. The lyrical approach is intriguing because he chose to focus more on music itself rather than love for any particular subject. The song is simply a ballad to music. This song was 1 of 2 of Caydn's works that were submitted into the 2021 NAfME Composer's Competition. One respectable commentary made by the judges was an amazement at his outstanding lyric-writing abilities.
